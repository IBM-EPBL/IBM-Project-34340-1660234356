Chronic Kidney Disease Detection




Overview

    Chronic kidney disease, also called chronic kidney failure, involves a gradual loss of kidney function.
    Your kidneys filter wastes and excess fluids from your blood, which are then removed in your urine. Advanced
    chronic kidney disease can cause dangerous levels of fluid, electrolytes and wastes to build up in your body.



Treatment

Medication helps manage symptoms. In later stages, filtering the blood with a machine (dialysis) or a transplant may be required.
